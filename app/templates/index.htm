What is VEEP?

VEEP stands for Volunteering Engineering Experience Program. We work with not-for-profits to build the next cohort of engineering students! Working with us means you are looking for...

Community Engagement

You'd love the chance to make a difference in your local community. You bring a balance of impact and empathy to the volunteering community. But you also want...

Learning Opportunities

You want to explore engineering across different disciplines -- and learn more about them, in a "doing" context! Classes are amazing for building fundamentals, but now it's time to build off of theory into practice. But you're an engineering hopeful, so you definitely want...

Experience

If you don't want to branch out, that's fine too! VEEP supports a wide variety of projects so you'll be able to accumulate professional know-how before you enter the workforce. Employers like 4.0's but they love work experience!

How It Works

I am a(n) :

Team members are the bread and butter of VEEP. We depend on You to make up our engineering teams! As a student you will be expected to contribute towards a project for either 1 or 2 semesters, keeping track of progress and communicating with your Not-for-Profit clients and Project Managers along the way.

Your timeline will look a little something like this (double the time length for Weeks 3~10) :

  • Week 1: Meet with your team, Project Manager, and Not-for-Profit. Develop preliminary project requirements and discuss between your team which features build into the Minimal Viable Product (MVP) that your client can accept.
  • Week 2~3: Revise, plan, and begin executing your project requirements. Your Project Manager will outline what we call a Rolling Schedule, and how they plan to keep development fast-paced and continuous throughout the school year.
  • Week 4~7: Implement the MVP that you defined in the earlier weeks! We don't expect you to completely finish all aspects of the project! Many of our projects are quite large in scope and often have additional features to work towards. We understand that there are other commitments during the school year which can impact your availability to work on your VEEP project. However, it is expected that you stay Accountable and plan ahead. VEEP will treat lack of communication as a sign that you no longer wish to participate and you will no longer be able to work on your project or sign-up for other VEEP opportunities in the future.
  • Week 8~9: Clean-up and polish your MVP. Did you do a poor job documenting the process to meet deadlines? Use this 2-week period to fix any minor issues that were in the prototype's design. If you wouldn't buy your own project as a product or service then it is not polished enough!
  • Week 10~12: Work towards those additional features! Congratulations! At this point you already have a fully-functional and robust product. If you wanted to end here you technically could, but we really encourage students to further extend their projects to meet the stretch goals designed for these features.
  • Week 13~14: Assemble your documentation and prepare for the Year End Project Showcase. This is your chance to show Not-for-Profits that you are good enough for professional work! The Year End Project Showcase is a new initiative we began to show off VEEP's technical rigour and creativity. Even if your project isn't finished at this point, we'd still like you to present your engineering processes and your approaches to solving problems!
